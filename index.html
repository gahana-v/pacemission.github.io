What does PACE do?

PACE will help scientists see how healthy the ocean is by checking on tiny plants
called phytoplankton, which are food for lots of sea creatures.
It will also help us understand air quality and how clouds affect the Earth's climate.


Why is PACE important?

The ocean is super important because so many people and animals depend on it.
PACE will help scientists track changes like harmful algal blooms, which can
be bad for fish and people.Plus, by studying aerosols (tiny particles like dust or smoke)
and clouds,PACE will help us better understand and predict weather, climate, and air quality.


How does PACE do this?

PACE uses special tools to see colors in the ocean and measure light.
These tools are really advanced and can give scientists more details than ever before!
It can also study things in the atmosphere, like aerosols and clouds,
and show how they interact with the ocean.


Why study the ocean and atmosphere together?

The ocean and the air are connected in many ways. For example, some tiny
particles in the air can help plankton grow, and plankton can help form clouds.
PACE will help scientists see how all these parts work together to better understand our planet!
